Case Study

INTEGIRLS Toronto — Math Competitions & Community

Designing experiences that empower gender minorities in STEM through math contests and community-building initiatives.

The Challenge

Math competitions often lack representation and welcoming spaces for gender minorities. Students reported feeling excluded, overlooked, or unsupported in traditional STEM events, limiting both participation and confidence.

My Role & Tools

Community engagement, event design, outreach strategy; Tools: Figma, Notion, Canva.

Audience

Middle and high school students from gender minority groups in STEM, teachers encouraging participation, and university mentors supporting inclusivity in math enrichment.

Process

  • Researched barriers to participation by interviewing students and educators.
  • Developed inclusive event structures (team-based contests, open difficulty levels, collaborative rounds).
  • Designed accessible registration flows and promotional materials with inclusive language.
  • Iterated on feedback from participants to refine competition design and community events.

Final Implementation

We launched INTEGIRLS Toronto’s first competition series with a focus on collaboration, accessibility, and representation. The event branding highlighted inclusivity, and the competition format balanced challenge with approachability. Outreach efforts expanded awareness among schools and community groups across Toronto.

Outcomes

Our competitions saw a significant increase in participation from gender minorities compared to traditional math contests. Feedback highlighted stronger confidence, a sense of belonging, and excitement to continue in STEM pathways.

Next Steps & Reflection

Future competitions will explore hybrid participation (in-person + online) to expand accessibility. We also aim to build mentorship pipelines with university students and professionals in STEM fields.

This project reinforced my belief in the power of inclusive design—not just in digital products but in educational experiences that shape confidence and opportunity.
