Case Study

Unifile LegalTech — Competition Project

Streamlining legal document management and accessibility for students and professionals.

The Challenge

Law students and early-career professionals often face scattered resources, inconsistent document management, and accessibility barriers when collaborating or preparing for competitions.

My Role & Tools

Product design, user research, prototyping; Tools: Figma, Notion, Miro.

Audience

Law students, educators, and early-stage legal professionals who need a centralized, accessible platform for organizing documents and sharing knowledge.

Process

  • Interviewed law students and competition participants to understand workflow pain points.
  • Mapped existing tools (Google Drive, PDFs, scattered resources) to identify inefficiencies.
  • Developed user flows for streamlined case prep and secure document sharing.
  • Prototyped a unified platform with search, tagging, and role-based permissions.

Final Mock up

The redesigned Unifile platform unifies scattered legal workflows into one accessible hub. With a simplified dashboard, clear document organization, and built-in collaboration tools, lawyers and clients can focus less on searching and more on solving cases. The final interface emphasizes clarity, trust, and efficiency, which are core values for a modern legaltech solution.

Outcomes

Created a functional prototype that reduces prep time, improves collaboration, and increases accessibility for competition teams and legal educators. Pitched submission (below) to discuss improvements and potential for growth.

Next Steps & Reflection

We plan to validate the platform through usability testing with more student teams and legal mentors. Future iterations will include AI-powered search, integrations with citation managers, and accessibility features for diverse learners.

This project not only improved my product thinking but also reinforced the importance of designing for inclusivity and real-world workflows.
